A time-course analysis of the oligodendrocyte translatome in response to spinal cord injury







This project utilizes RiboTag technology to isolate oligodendrocyte-specific, ribosome-associated mRNAs to investigate changes in the oligodendrocyte translatome in the spinal cord before and after progressive stages of a moderate thoracic spinal cord contusion injury. This dataset provides information about how oligodendrocytes respond to an injury at the translational level (ribosome-associated mRNAs) to identify novel mechanisms by which oligodendrocytes help or hinder the healing process after a devastating injury.




Translatome: transcriptomics of isolated ribosome-associated mRNAs. These are considered “actively translating”.
